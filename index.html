Intro

Hello. Welcome to my NC Name Change Packet. This was something I wish I had going through this process. Hopefully it will save you some of the struggling I needed to go through to find all these forms.

For context, I went through this process while legally transitioning as an adult (the process is different for people under 18) and living in Wake county. I am not a lawyer, and so I cannot give legal advice (although here are the general statutes on name changes, G.S. 101, if you are interested). That said, I am somebody who had to stumble through this complicated process. My hope is that this guide will help make this process (slightly) less complicated for you.

Background Checks

For a name change, you will need to get a state and a federal background check, and you will need to get fingerprinted for each.


National Identity History Summary

This is your federal background check. It is fastest to complete it on line. After submitting your request, you will get instuction for how to submit you fingerprints for this background check.

Fingerprinting - Federal

Fingerprinting for federal background checks can be done at some post offices. You will need to do this after requesting your National Identity History Summary, as you will need yoor order confirmation number to register to get fingerprinted at the post office.

*You can skip this step by getting a second fingerprint card while getting your fingerprints for your state background check. However, you will have to mail that second fingerprint card, which will cause the federal background check to take much longer.

Fingerprinting - State

You will need to find this on your own (sorry) as it varies be county (but if you are in Wake county, you can get fingerprinted here). You should get a fingerprint card from here that you will need to send with your Right to Review request.

Right to Review

This is your request for a state background check (found here). You will need to mail this with with your fingerprint card and either a certified check or money order after you get your National Identity History Summary results back.

Court Documents

Now that you have your background checks' results, you can actually legally change your name. These are the documents that you will actually file with the court. You can find your courthouse here on the NC Courts website. This is also the site where all these forms can be found (here).


Notice of Intent to Change Name

You will need to post this at courthouse at least 10 days before you submit your name change.

Affidavit of Good Character (x2)

You will need two people (preferably not blood relatives) from your country to fill this out, stating how they know you and how you are of good character. As you have not yet changed your name yet, they should use your deadname when talking about you for this form.

Application For Adult Name Change

Self Explanatory, this is the actual application for a name change. You will need to submit this with:

  • You 10-day old Notice of Intent to Change Name
  • 2 Affidavit of Good Character
  • The results of both of your background checks
You will also need to have:
  • A form of ID
  • Your Birth Certificate
  • A form of payment for the filing fee
If you don't feel comfortable mentioning your transness as your reason for changing your name (I didn't), you can say something to the effect of your "new" name being how people gernally refer to you and that you would like you legal documentation to match (which is true). From what I have read, as long as your are not changing your name to commit fraud, they are pretty accepting of your resoning.

ID Documents

Now that you have your court order, you need to go about updating all you ID documents.


Social Security Card

This was one of the easier documents to update. You can find your local social security office here. You will need to bring your court order. You will need to do it a few days before updating your driver's license since the social security database is where the DMV gets their data from. You can also change your sex marker without any other documentation.

US Passport

You will need to send you court order to change your name. At the same time, you can update your gender marker without any other documentation (as of March 2024).

Driver's license

You will want to make an appointment ASAP here. For the name change, you will need to bring your court order proving you name change. For your sex marker change, you need to bring an updated passport with the correct information on it (OR this form filled out, but the updated passport is much easier).

Voter Registration

You can do this while you are updating your driver's license.

Birth Certificate
